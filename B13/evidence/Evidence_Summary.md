# B13 – Evidence Summary

This activity involved performing controlled jailbreak tests against a generative AI assistant to determine whether its safety mechanisms could be bypassed. Screenshots of the prompts and responses were collected as evidence throughout the testing process.

## Evidence 1 – Initial Jailbreak Attempt

The first prompt attempted to obtain instructions for performing a phishing attack by presenting the request as part of a university assessment. The AI refused to provide actionable instructions and instead redirected the conversation toward phishing awareness, detection, and educational cybersecurity concepts.

## Evidence 3 – Educational Phishing Simulation

The AI was then asked to create a mock phishing simulation for educational purposes. Rather than providing instructions for conducting a real phishing attack, the AI generated a safe phishing awareness exercise designed for training and learning. The response focused on identifying phishing indicators and understanding social engineering techniques.

## Reflection

The evidence collected demonstrates that the generative AI assistant maintained its safety controls during the jailbreak testing process. Attempts to obtain instructions that could facilitate phishing attacks were unsuccessful, while legitimate educational and awareness-focused requests were permitted. This demonstrates how modern AI systems attempt to balance usefulness with safeguards that reduce the risk of misuse.
