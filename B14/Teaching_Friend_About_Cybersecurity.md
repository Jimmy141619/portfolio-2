# B14 – Teach Your Friends About a Cybersecurity Topic

## Introduction

Cybersecurity awareness is important for protecting individuals from online threats. This activity involved teaching a friend about ransomware and phishing attacks, including how they work, how to recognize them, and how to reduce the risk of becoming a victim.

---

# Topic Chosen

Ransomware and Phishing Attacks

---

# Objective

The objective of this activity was to improve cybersecurity awareness by explaining two common cyber threats and providing practical advice for identifying and avoiding them.

---

# Teaching Activity

I conducted a discussion with my cousin Tyson through text messages.

The discussion began with an explanation of ransomware. I explained that ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment in exchange for restoring access. I discussed common signs of a ransomware infection, including inaccessible files, unexpected file changes, ransom notes, and unusual device behaviour.

I also explained common methods used to spread ransomware, such as malicious email attachments, phishing links, infected downloads, and compromised websites.

The discussion then moved to phishing emails. I explained that phishing emails are designed to trick users into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware. Common warning signs such as urgent language, suspicious links, requests for passwords, and unexpected attachments were discussed.

---

# Knowledge Check

To assess understanding, I asked three True/False questions about phishing attacks.

### Question 1

True or False:

A phishing email is designed to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or banking details.

Answer: True

### Question 2

True or False:

It is safe to click links in emails from unknown senders if the message looks urgent.

Answer: False

### Question 3

True or False:

Poor spelling, suspicious links, and requests for personal information can be warning signs of a phishing email.

Answer: True

The questions were used to reinforce the concepts discussed during the teaching activity.

---

# Outcome

The discussion helped explain the risks associated with ransomware and phishing attacks and provided practical guidance for identifying suspicious emails and avoiding common cyber threats. The knowledge check demonstrated an understanding of the key concepts covered during the discussion.

---

# Reflection

This activity demonstrated the importance of cybersecurity awareness and education. Teaching another person about ransomware and phishing attacks reinforced my own understanding of the topics while helping someone else learn how to identify and avoid common cyber threats. The activity highlighted how simple awareness measures can significantly reduce cybersecurity risks.
