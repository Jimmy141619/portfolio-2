# B14 – Evidence Summary

This activity involved teaching a friend about ransomware and phishing attacks through a text-based discussion. The objective was to improve cybersecurity awareness by explaining how these threats work, how to recognize them, and how to respond safely.

## Evidence 1 – Explaining Ransomware

I explained what ransomware is and how it affects victims by encrypting files and demanding payment for their recovery. The discussion covered common signs of a ransomware infection, including inaccessible files, ransom notes, and unusual computer behaviour.

I also discussed methods attackers commonly use to spread ransomware and explained preventative measures such as software updates, antivirus protection, safe downloading practices, and maintaining backups.

## Evidence 2 – Explaining Phishing Emails

The discussion continued with an explanation of phishing emails. I explained that phishing emails are designed to trick users into clicking malicious links, downloading malware, or revealing sensitive information such as passwords and banking details.

Common phishing warning signs were discussed, including:

* Urgent messages demanding immediate action
* Requests for passwords or personal information
* Suspicious links
* Poor spelling and grammar
* Unexpected attachments

## Evidence 3 – Knowledge Check

To assess understanding, I asked three True/False questions relating to phishing attacks:

1. A phishing email is designed to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or banking details.
2. It is safe to click links in emails from unknown senders if the message looks urgent.
3. Poor spelling, suspicious links, and requests for personal information can be warning signs of a phishing email.

The participant correctly answered all three questions, demonstrating an understanding of the cybersecurity concepts discussed during the activity.

## Reflection

The teaching activity successfully introduced the concepts of ransomware and phishing attacks using simple explanations and practical examples. The participant actively engaged in the discussion and correctly answered all knowledge-check questions. This activity demonstrated the importance of cybersecurity awareness and how education can help individuals identify and avoid common cyber threats.
