# B28 – Evidence Summary

This activity involved creating a cyber safety flyer targeted at university students. The flyer was designed to raise awareness of common cybersecurity threats and provide practical advice that students can apply to improve their online safety.

## Evidence 1 – Flyer Design

A cyber safety flyer was created using an infographic-style layout. The flyer focused on eight key cybersecurity topics relevant to university students:

* Strong Passwords
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Phishing Awareness
* Software Updates
* Secure Browsing
* Safe Downloads
* Data Backups
* Thinking Before Clicking Links and Attachments

The information was presented using concise explanations and actionable recommendations to make the content easy to understand.

## Evidence 2 – Targeted Cybersecurity Awareness

The flyer was specifically designed for university students, who regularly use online learning systems, email services, cloud storage, and social media platforms. The advice included in the flyer addressed common risks faced by students and encouraged safe online behaviours.

## Reflection

This activity demonstrated how cybersecurity information can be effectively communicated through visual resources. The flyer provided practical and accessible advice that can help university students reduce their exposure to common cyber threats. Creating the flyer reinforced the importance of cybersecurity awareness and user education as part of an overall security strategy.
