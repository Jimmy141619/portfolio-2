# B28 – Produce a Cyber Safety Flyer

## Introduction

Cybersecurity awareness is an important part of staying safe online. University students frequently use email, online learning platforms, cloud storage services, and social media, making them common targets for cyber threats such as phishing attacks, malware, and account compromise.

This activity involved designing a cyber safety flyer targeted at university students.

---

# Target Audience

University Students

---

# Objective

The objective of the flyer was to provide simple and practical cybersecurity advice that students can use to improve their online safety and reduce their exposure to common cyber threats.

---

# Flyer Content

The flyer covered eight key cybersecurity topics:

1. Strong Passwords
2. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
3. Phishing Awareness
4. Software Updates
5. Secure Browsing
6. Safe Downloads
7. Data Backups
8. Thinking Before Clicking Links and Attachments

Each topic was presented as a short and actionable cybersecurity recommendation that students can apply in their everyday use of technology.

---

# Design Process

The flyer was designed using a visual infographic format. Information was organized into clearly labelled sections around a central cybersecurity theme to improve readability and engagement.

The design used concise text and simple language to ensure the information was easy to understand and remember.

---

# Outcome

The completed flyer provides university students with practical guidance for improving their cybersecurity practices. The information highlights common risks and promotes safe online behaviours that can help prevent cyber incidents.

---

# Reflection

Creating the flyer demonstrated the importance of communicating cybersecurity concepts in a simple and accessible format. By presenting cybersecurity advice visually and using clear language, the flyer can help improve awareness and encourage safer online behaviour among university students.
