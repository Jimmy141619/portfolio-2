# B15 – Evidence Summary

This activity involved teaching an elderly family member about website security, HTTPS, encryption, and digital certificates through a text-based discussion. The objective was to improve cybersecurity awareness and help them understand how to identify secure websites when browsing the internet.

## Evidence 1 – Introduction to HTTPS

I explained the difference between HTTP and HTTPS and described how HTTPS protects information sent between a user's device and a website through encryption. I also discussed the importance of checking for the padlock symbol before entering sensitive information such as passwords or banking details.

## Evidence 2 – Understanding Digital Certificates

I explained how websites use digital certificates issued by trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs). The discussion covered how browsers verify certificates and how users can trust websites that have been validated by a recognized third party.

To simplify the concept, I used a passport verification analogy, explaining that Certificate Authorities act as trusted organizations that verify a website's identity.

## Evidence 3 – Knowledge Check

After the lesson, I asked three True/False questions to test understanding of the material:

1. If a website uses HTTPS, information sent between your device and the website is encrypted.
2. The padlock symbol means the website's identity has been verified by a trusted Certificate Authority.
3. It is safe to enter banking information on any website, even if it only uses HTTP and shows "Not Secure."

The participant correctly answered all three questions, demonstrating an understanding of the concepts discussed during the lesson.

## Reflection

The teaching activity successfully introduced HTTPS, encryption, website certificates, and Certificate Authorities in a simple and understandable way. The participant was able to apply the information during the knowledge check and correctly identify the key security concepts. This activity demonstrated the value of cybersecurity education in helping individuals make safer decisions online.
