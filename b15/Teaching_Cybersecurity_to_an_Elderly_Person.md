# B15 – Teach an Elderly Person About a Cybersecurity Topic

## Introduction

Cybersecurity awareness is important for people of all ages, particularly older adults who are often targeted by online scams, phishing attacks, and fraudulent websites. This activity involved teaching an elderly family member about website security, HTTPS, and digital certificates.

---

# Topic Chosen

Understanding HTTPS, Website Security, and Digital Certificates

---

# Objective

The objective of this activity was to explain how secure websites protect users online and how to identify whether a website can be trusted before entering sensitive information.

---

# Teaching Activity

I conducted a discussion with my grandmother through text messages and explained the difference between HTTP and HTTPS.

The discussion covered:

* What HTTPS means
* Why secure websites use encryption
* The importance of the padlock symbol in web browsers
* How digital certificates work
* How trusted third-party organizations verify website identities

I explained that HTTPS helps protect information sent between a user's device and a website by encrypting the communication. I also explained that websites obtain digital certificates from trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs), which act as independent organizations that verify website ownership.

To help explain the concept, I used the analogy of a passport or identification document, where a trusted authority verifies someone's identity and others rely on that verification.

---

# Knowledge Check

To confirm understanding, I asked the following questions:

### Question 1

True or False:

If a website uses HTTPS, information sent between your device and the website is encrypted.

Answer: True

### Question 2

True or False:

The padlock symbol means the website's identity has been verified by a trusted Certificate Authority.

Answer: True

### Question 3

True or False:

It is safe to enter banking information on any website, even if it only uses HTTP and shows "Not Secure."

Answer: False

The questions were used to reinforce the concepts discussed during the teaching activity and assess understanding.

---

# Outcome

The discussion helped explain how secure websites protect user information and why HTTPS should be used when entering passwords, banking information, or other sensitive data. The knowledge check demonstrated an understanding of the key concepts discussed.

---

# Reflection

This activity demonstrated the importance of cybersecurity education for older adults. Teaching website security concepts using simple language and relatable examples made the topic easier to understand. The activity highlighted how awareness and education can help individuals make safer decisions online and reduce the risk of becoming victims of cybercrime.
